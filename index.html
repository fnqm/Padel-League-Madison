02 / Regular Season Format

Regular Season Format

  • 3 sets per match
  • Partners alternate every set
  • Players for each match and set are selected randomly — the organizer may make minor adjustments to minimize repeated combinations
  • Players must track games won and lost
  • One player from each match must update the score on the Google Sheet provided

  • 📊 Rankings are based on total games won (not sets won). Tiebreaker: sets won.
  • 📈 Eff% = Points ÷ Total Games Played. Example: Win 6-3, 7-6, lose 4-6 → Eff% = 17/32 = 53%
03 / Scoring Rules

Scoring Rules

  • Deuce: one deuce only, followed by a Golden Point
  • 6–6 in a set: standard tiebreak to 7, win by 2
04 / Finals

Finals Format

Finals are seeded based on regular-season rankings.

🏆
1st–4th
Top 4 Match
🎾
5th–8th
Bottom 4 Match
  • Top 4: Best 4 players compete together
  • Bottom 4: Other 4 players compete together
  • Format: best of 3 sets with rotating partners
05 / Substitution Policy

Substitution Policy

  • Notify the group chat at least 3 days in advance if you cannot attend
  • Finding a substitute is the responsibility of the absent player
  • The group chat includes people willing to sub — share date and time to find a replacement
  • If no sub is found, the match will be rescheduled as a last resort

02 / Individual Ranking Format

Individual Ranking

  • Each player accumulates points from every game they play
  • 📈 Eff% is the primary ranking driver — calculated as Games Won divided by Total Games Played
03 / Scoring Rules

Scoring Rules

  • Match format: each player serves twice · 8 service games per match in total
  • Night total: 3 matches per Tuesday = 24 games played per player
  • Deuce: the first two deuces are played out normally (advantage in/out) · if a third deuce is reached, a single Golden Point decides the game immediately
  • Substitutes: games played by a substitute do not count toward the absent player's score — only games played in person are recorded
04 / Season Split After Week 4

Season Split

After 4 weeks, players are ranked and split into two leagues for the remaining 4 weeks.

🔥
Top 8
Upper League
🎾
Bottom 8
Lower League
  • Upper League (Top 8): compete among themselves — Apr 28 through May 19
  • Lower League (Bottom 8): compete among themselves simultaneously
  • Final standings are determined separately within each league
